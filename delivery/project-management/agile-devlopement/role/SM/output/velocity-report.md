This report tracks the team's completed story points across recent sprints to understand delivery capacity and predict future planning.

## 3. Average Velocity

Average completed velocity over last 4 sprints:

`(20 + 21 + 22 + 21) / 4 = 21`

**Average Velocity: 21 Story Points per Sprint**

---

## 4. Trend Observation

- Team velocity is relatively stable around **21 story points**
- No major fluctuation across recent sprints
- This suggests the team has a predictable delivery capacity

---

## 5. Interpretation

### What this means
- The team can usually commit to about **20–21 story points per sprint**
- Planning significantly above this level may increase carry-over risk
- Stable velocity indicates healthy sprint planning and team consistency

---

## 6. Risks / Notes

- Velocity may drop if:
  - requirements are unclear
  - blockers are unresolved
  - team capacity changes
- Velocity should be used for **team planning**, not individual performance evaluation

---

## 7. Recommended Planning Guidance

- Recommended sprint commitment: **20–21 story points**
- If a sprint contains high uncertainty or external dependency, reduce planned points
- Recalculate average velocity periodically (for example, every 3–5 sprints)

---

## 8. One-Line Summary

The team currently has a stable delivery capacity of approximately **21 story points per sprint**, which can be used as a reference for future sprint planning.
