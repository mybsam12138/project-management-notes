# Pre-sales Engineer Summary

## 1. Definition

A **Pre-sales Engineer** (or Solution Engineer / Solution Architect in some companies) is responsible for:

> Translating customer business needs into feasible technical solutions and helping the company win the deal.

---

## 2. Core Responsibilities

### 2.1 Understand Customer Needs
- Identify real problems behind customer requirements
- Ask clarifying questions
- Distinguish between *stated needs* and *actual needs*

---

### 2.2 Design Solution
- Provide high-level architecture
- Evaluate multiple solution options (build vs buy vs integrate)
- Balance:
  - cost
  - timeline
  - risk
  - scalability

---

### 2.3 Support Sales Process
- Participate in client meetings
- Explain solutions in a clear and business-friendly way
- Handle technical objections

---

### 2.4 Produce Deliverables
- Solution proposal documents
- Architecture diagrams
- Cost and timeline estimation
- Demo / PoC (Proof of Concept)

---

### 2.5 Align Internally
- Work with:
  - Tech Lead / Architects → feasibility
  - PM → scope & delivery
  - Sales → pricing & strategy

---

## 3. Key Outputs

Typical deliverables include:

- Solution Proposal
- High-level Architecture Design
- Implementation Plan
- Cost Estimation
- Risk Analysis
- Demo / PoC

---

## 4. Key Skills

### 4.1 Technical Breadth
- Understand system architecture
- Familiar with common tools and platforms
- Able to evaluate different technical approaches

---

### 4.2 Solution Thinking
- Not just "how to build"
- But:
  - whether to build
  - what to build
  - what is optimal

---

### 4.3 Communication Skills
- Explain complex concepts simply
- Adapt communication to:
  - business stakeholders
  - technical teams

---

### 4.4 Business Awareness
- Understand:
  - cost vs value
  - ROI (Return on Investment)
  - risk trade-offs

---

## 5. KPI / Performance Metrics

Pre-sales is typically evaluated by:

- Deal conversion rate
- Revenue contribution
- Quality of solution proposals
- Customer feedback
- Support to sales effectiveness

---

## 6. Position in Project Lifecycle

Pre-sales operates mainly in:

Opportunity / Pre-sales Phase

But may also:
- Support during major scope changes
- Re-engage after delivery for new opportunities

---

## 7. Relationship with Other Roles

| Role        | Focus                     |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Sales       | Business / revenue        |
| Pre-sales   | Solution / feasibility    |
| PM          | Delivery / execution      |
| Tech Lead   | Technical implementation  |
| Support     | Post-delivery stability   |

---

## 8. Key Differences from Other Roles

### Pre-sales vs Tech Lead
- Pre-sales: Why and what to build
- Tech Lead: How to build

### Pre-sales vs PM
- Pre-sales: Define solution before contract
- PM: Manage delivery after contract

---

## 9. Core Mindset

> A pre-sales engineer is not only solving technical problems,  
but deciding the best way to solve business problems and making the solution sellable.

---

## 10. One-line Summary

> Pre-sales bridges business and technology to turn opportunities into successful projects.
