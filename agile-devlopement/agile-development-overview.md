# Agile Development Summary

## 1. What is Agile Development?

Agile development is a **software development approach** that emphasizes:

* iterative delivery
* flexibility
* customer collaboration
* continuous improvement

> Instead of delivering everything at once, Agile focuses on delivering small, working increments frequently.

---

## 2. Agile Manifesto (Core Values)

Defined in the Agile Manifesto:

### 🔹 Four Key Values

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan

---

## 3. Key Characteristics of Agile

### 🔁 Iterative Development

* Work is delivered in small cycles (iterations or sprints)

---

### 🚀 Frequent Delivery

* Deliver usable software regularly (e.g., every 1–2 weeks)

---

### 🤝 Collaboration

* Close communication between team and stakeholders

---

### 🔄 Adaptability

* Adjust requirements based on feedback and changes

---

### 👥 Cross-functional Teams

* Teams include all skills needed to deliver work

---

## 4. Common Agile Frameworks

* Scrum → most widely used
* Kanban → focuses on flow and WIP limits
* Extreme Programming (XP) → focuses on engineering practices

---

## 5. Agile vs Traditional (Waterfall)

| Aspect      | Agile                   | Waterfall  |
| ----------- | ----------------------- | ---------- |
| Approach    | Iterative               | Sequential |
| Delivery    | Frequent                | One-time   |
| Flexibility | High                    | Low        |
| Feedback    | Continuous              | Late       |
| Risk        | Lower (early detection) | Higher     |

---

## 6. Benefits of Agile

* Faster feedback and delivery
* Better alignment with customer needs
* Improved product quality
* Increased team collaboration
* Reduced risk

---

## 7. Challenges of Agile

* Requires experienced and disciplined teams
* Needs strong communication
* Scope may change frequently
* Harder to predict long-term timeline

---

## 8. Relationship with Scrum

* Agile = **mindset / philosophy**
* Scrum = **framework to implement Agile**

---

## 9. Key Principles (Simplified)

* Deliver working software frequently
* Welcome changing requirements
* Collaborate closely with stakeholders
* Build sustainable development pace
* Continuously improve processes

---

## 10. Interview Summary

> Agile development is an iterative and flexible approach to software development that focuses on delivering working software frequently, collaborating with customers, and adapting to change. It is guided by the Agile Manifesto and implemented through frameworks like Scrum.
