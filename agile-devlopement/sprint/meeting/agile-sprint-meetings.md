# Agile Sprint (2 Weeks) – Meetings Summary (with Timing & Duration)

## Overview

In a typical 2-week Agile sprint, key meetings are scheduled at specific times with defined durations to ensure alignment, progress tracking, feedback, and continuous improvement.

---

## 1. Sprint Planning

### Timing
- Day 1 of Sprint (start)

### Duration
- 2–4 hours (for a 2-week sprint)

### Participants
- Product Owner (PO)
- Tech Lead
- Developers (FE/BE)
- QA

### Purpose
- Define sprint goal
- Select backlog items
- Estimate effort
- Break down tasks

### Output
- Sprint backlog
- Sprint goal
- Task breakdown (Jira tickets)

---

## 2. Daily Stand-up (Daily Scrum)

### Timing
- Every day (same time, e.g. 10:00 AM)

### Duration
- 10–15 minutes

### Participants
- Developers
- QA
- Tech Lead (optional)
- PO (optional)

### Purpose
- Share progress (yesterday / today)
- Identify blockers
- Align daily work

### Output
- Updated task status
- Identified blockers

---

## 3. Backlog Refinement (Grooming)

### Timing
- Mid-sprint (e.g. Day 5–7)
- Or 1–2 days before next sprint

### Duration
- 1–2 hours

### Participants
- Product Owner
- Tech Lead
- Developers

### Purpose
- Clarify upcoming requirements
- Split large stories
- Prepare for next sprint planning

### Output
- Refined backlog
- Clear user stories
- Initial estimates

---

## 4. Sprint Review (Demo)

### Timing
- Last day of sprint (or second last day)

### Duration
- 1–2 hours

### Participants
- Product Owner
- Stakeholders / Customers
- Developers
- QA

### Purpose
- Demonstrate completed work
- Collect feedback
- Validate business value

### Output
- Feedback list
- Accepted / rejected features
- Change requests

---

## 5. Sprint Retrospective

### Timing
- After Sprint Review (same day)

### Duration
- 1–1.5 hours

### Participants
- Entire team (PO, Dev, QA, Lead)

### Purpose
- Reflect on process
- Identify improvements
- Discuss team collaboration

### Output
- Action items
- Process improvements

---

## Typical 2-Week Sprint Timeline

Week 1:
- Day 1: Sprint Planning
- Daily: Stand-ups
- Mid-week: Backlog Refinement

Week 2:
- Daily: Stand-ups
- Day 9–10: Sprint Review
- Day 10: Retrospective

---

## Summary

A well-structured sprint includes:

- Planning → define work (Day 1)
- Daily sync → track progress (daily)
- Refinement → prepare future work (mid-sprint)
- Review → validate outcome (end)
- Retrospective → improve process (end)

These meetings ensure continuous delivery, feedback, and improvement.
